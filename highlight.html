Bagan

Bagan is an ancient city located in the Mandalay Region of Myanmar. From the 9th to 13th centuries, the city was the capital of the Pagan Kingdom, the first kingdom that unified the regions that would later constitute modern Myanmar. During the kingdom's height between the 11th and 13th centuries, over 10,000 Buddhist temples, pagodas and monasteries were constructed in the Bagan plains alone, of which the remains of over 2,200 temples and pagodas still survive to the present day.

Area:104 km²
Founded:mid-to-late 9th century
Number of airports:1

U Bein Bridge

U Bein Bridge is a crossing that spans the Taungthaman Lake near Amarapura in Myanmar. The 1.2-kilometre bridge was built around 1850 and is believed to be the oldest and (once) longest teakwood bridge in the world. Construction began when the capital of Ava Kingdom moved to Amarapura, and the bridge is named after the mayor who had it built. It is particularly busy during July and August when the lake is at its highest.

Locate:Amarapura Township, Myanmar
Design:Wooden Foot Bridge (Teak Bridge)
Total length:3,967 ft (1,209 m) or 482 spans or 0.75 miles (1.2 km) (total bridge) (1086 posts)
Construction start:1849
Construction end:1851

Chin State

Chin State hkyang is a state in western Myanmar. The 36,019-square-kilometre (13,907 sq mi) Chin State is bordered by Sagaing Division and Magway Division to the east, Rakhine State to the south, Bangladesh to the south-west, and the Indian states of Mizoram to the west and Manipur to the north. The capital of the state is Hakha. The state is a mountainous region with few transportation links. Chin State is sparsely populated and remains one of the least developed areas of the country. There are 53 different subtribe and languages in Chin State.

Capital:Hakha
Total Area:36,018.8 km2
Total Pupulation:478,801
Ethnicities:Chin

Kalaw Trekking

Established by British civil servants as a summer resort, Kalaw retains much of its colonial feel. The cool air and cared-for streets make Kalaw holidays ideal for those wishing to take it easy and soak up the scenery. Kalaw has also come to be known as a trekkers' paradise, with a list of fascinating and challenging multi-day hikes across the surrounding landscape ensuring chances to meet local hill tribes and find high-altitude lakes. Kalaw is in Shan State.

Districts:Taunggyi District
Township:Kalaw Township
Division:Shan State
Religions:Buddhism

Inle Lake

Inle Lake is in the Shan Hills of Myanmar. At its southwestern edge, Phaung Daw Oo Paya is a tiered pagoda with gilded Buddha statues. In the hills, near the market town of Indein, the stupas of Shwe Inn Dein Pagoda resemble a field of gold spires. The ruined stupas at nearby Nyaung Ohak have been reclaimed by forest. Maing Thauk village is split between land and water, with a wooden bridge connecting the two halves.

Area:116.3 km²
Surface elevation: 884 m
Location:Shan State
Max. depth:12 ft (3.7 m) (dry season; +5 ft in monsoon season)

Shwedagon Pagoda

The Shwedagon Pagoda, officially named Shwedagon Zedi Daw and also known as the Great Dagon Pagoda and the Golden Pagoda, is a gilded stupa located in Yangon, Myanmar. The 326-foot-tall pagoda is situated on Singuttara Hill, to the west of Kandawgyi Lake, and dominates the Yangon skyline.

Address:Bahan, Yangon
Height:105 m
Opened:1372
Architect:Okkalapa of Burma
Opening Hours:04:00-22:00 hrs

Kyaik Htee Yoo

Kyaiktiyo Pagoda is a well-known Buddhist pilgrimage site in Mon State, Burma. It is a small pagoda (7.3 metres (24 ft)) built on the top of a granite boulder covered with gold leaves pasted on by its male devotees. According to legend, the Golden Rock itself is precariously perched on a strand of the Buddha's hair. The balancing rock seems to defy gravity, as it perpetually appears to be on the verge of rolling down the hill. The rock and the pagoda are at the top of Mt. Kyaiktiyo.

Region:Mon State
Elevation:‎1,100 m (3,609 ft)
Spire height‎:15 m (49 ft)
Sect:Theravada Buddhism
Municipality: Kyaikto

Inn Wa

Inwa is an ancient city near Mandalay, in central Myanmar (formerly Burma). It's known for religious structures left over from several reigns as the nation's capital, from the 14th to the 19th centuries. Dominating these is the Bagaya Kyaung, a 19th-century working monastery made of teak. The large, brick-and-stucco Maha Aungmye Bonzan monastery dates back to the 1800s. Its crypt area is a haven for bats.

Region: Mandalay Region
District:Kyaukse District)
Founded:26 February 1365
Ethnicities:Bamar
Religions:Theravada Buddhism
